<p><p>The fluorine refrigerants market comprises several types, including Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), Hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs), Hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), Hydrofluoroolefins (HFOs), and others. CFCs, once popular, are now largely phased out due to environmental concerns. HCFCs are transitional substitutes but are also being restricted. HFCs have gained use as lower ozone-depleting agents but contribute to global warming. HFOs represent a newer generation with low global warming potential, appealing to environmentally conscious regulations. Other refrigerants include natural alternatives and blends catering to diverse applications.</p></p>
