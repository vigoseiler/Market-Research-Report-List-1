<p><p>Automotive Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is an advanced emissions control technology used in diesel engines to reduce nitrogen oxides (NOx) emissions. The system works by injecting a urea-based solution, commonly known as diesel exhaust fluid (DEF), into the exhaust stream. This solution reacts with NOx in the presence of a catalyst, converting harmful gases into harmless nitrogen and water vapor. </p><p>The Automotive Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) System Market is expected to experience substantial growth, projected at a CAGR of 7% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by stringent government regulations aimed at reducing vehicular emissions, increasing demand for fuel-efficient vehicles, and the rising awareness of environmental sustainability. Furthermore, the growing adoption of SCR technology in commercial vehicles and light-duty trucks is contributing to market expansion. </p><p>Additionally, innovations in SCR systems, such as improved catalyst formulations and advanced control strategies, are enhancing performance and efficiency.
