<p><p>The full-frame camera market is primarily divided into three types: DSLR, mirrorless, and other formats. DSLR cameras utilize mechanical mirrors and optical viewfinders, known for their robust build and extensive lens options. Mirrorless cameras, on the other hand, offer a more compact design, rely on electronic viewfinders, and provide faster shooting speeds and advanced video capabilities. The "other" market includes niche options like medium format cameras and specialty devices, catering to specific professional needs and high-end enthusiasts seeking unique features.</p></p>
