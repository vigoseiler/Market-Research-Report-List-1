<p><p>The thoracic fixation systems market comprises three main types: metal, bioabsorbable materials, and ceramics. Metal systems, typically made from titanium or stainless steel, offer durability and strength for long-term support. Bioabsorbable materials provide a temporary fixation solution that gradually dissolves, reducing the need for additional surgeries. Ceramic options are known for their biocompatibility and ability to integrate with bone, making them suitable for specific patients. Each type serves distinct clinical needs in thoracic surgery and spinal stabilization.</p></p>
