Cationic resins are employed to capture positively charged ions and molecules, facilitating the purification of proteins and nucleic acids. Conversely, anionic resins target negatively charged species. Both types of resins play crucial roles in biopharmaceutical manufacturing, water treatment, and food processing, driving demand for effective separation technologies.</p></p>
