<p><p>The self-tying shoes market is divided into two primary types: rechargeable and non-rechargeable self-tying shoes. Rechargeable self-tying shoes utilize battery-powered mechanisms to automatically secure laces, allowing users to charge them for extended functionality. In contrast, non-rechargeable self-tying shoes rely on mechanical systems or elastic materials that require no power source, offering a simpler and often more affordable alternative. Both types cater to different consumer preferences, with rechargeable options highlighting advanced technology while non-rechargeable options emphasize practicality and ease of use.</p></p>
