<p><p>The small-scale liquefaction market primarily consists of two types: direct liquefaction and indirect liquefaction. In direct liquefaction, biomass or organic materials are converted into liquid fuels through thermal processes, resulting in a more straightforward and often cost-effective approach. Conversely, indirect liquefaction involves first converting feedstocks into gaseous fuels, which are then transformed into liquid fuels through chemical processes. Both methods cater to diverse energy needs and promote sustainable fuel production, reflecting growing interest in cleaner alternatives and resource efficiency.</p></p>
